# Website Plan: YouTube Channel & Projects Showcase

## 1. Goals & Purpose
*   **Central Hub:** A single place for your audience to find your YouTube videos, project deep-dives, interactive projects, and blog posts.
*   **Showcase Projects:** Detailed write-ups, screenshots, and code snippets for your technical projects. And a section for projects to explorer directly inside a subsite dedicated to that project.
*   **Content Ownership:** A platform you control entirely, separate from YouTube's algorithm.

## 2. Target Audience
*   YouTube subscribers looking for more in-depth content.
*   Fellow developers, creators, and potential collaborators or employers viewing your portfolio.

## 3. Proposed Sitemap (Pages)
*   **Home (`/`)**: 
    *   Hero section with a strong introduction and latest/featured YouTube video.
    *   Quick links to recent blog posts and top projects.
*   **Projects (`/projects`)**: 
    *   Grid/Gallery of your projects.
    *   Individual project pages (`/projects/[id]`) with in-depth descriptions, tech stack used, challenges faced, and links to source code/live demos.
*   **Blog/Videos (`/blog`)**: 
    *   Articles accompanying your YouTube videos (embedded video + text tutorial/thoughts).
    *   Standalone articles about programming, tech, or your journey.
*   **About (`/about`)**: 
    *   Who you are, your background, your setup, and how to contact you.

## 4. Design & Aesthetics
*   **Theme:** Modern, sleek, and premium.
*   **Color Palette:** Curated harmonious colors (e.g., deep dark mode by default with vibrant accent colors like neon blue/purple for tech vibes).
*   **Style:** Glassmorphism elements, subtle gradients, and clean typography (e.g., Inter or Outfit fonts).
*   **Interactivity:** Smooth micro-animations on hover, smooth page transitions, and a dynamic feel that "wows" the visitor.

## 5. Technology Stack
*Since no specific stack was requested yet, here are the recommendations based on standard practices:*
*   **Option A (Modern Web App / highly recommended for dynamic content):** Next.js or Vite (React) with Vanilla CSS (or Tailwind if preferred later) for styling. This is great for a scalable blog/portfolio.
*   **Option B (Simple & Fast):** Pure HTML, CSS (Vanilla), and JavaScript. Good for a static portfolio, but managing a blog might get tedious without a static site generator.
*   **Content Management:** MDX (Markdown with components) for writing blogs/projects directly in the codebase, or a headless CMS if you prefer a graphical editor later.
